How does it work?

Have you ever thought to yourself, why am I buying a lottery ticket? There is absolutely no chance that I'm going to win? Well we have, and then we thought, why not create a place where people can bet that no one will win, and that’s what we did.

But how does it work?

Smart people like you sign up to our site and deposit some money into their account wallet, they then decide if they think someone will match all 6 numbers in the UK national lottery draw. If you think they will then you bet 'Yay', and if you think they won't then you bet 'Nay'.

The site will show you the estimated return of a possible £10 bet for both the Yay or the Nay. Note that the return will vary depending on how many people agree or disagree with you or disagree before the draw closes.

When does the draw close?

The betting stops half an hour before the National Lottery draw on a Saturday night to give us time to work out the final numbers.

Then what happens?

If you were correct, either claiming that no one would win the jackpot and no one did, or that someone would win and they did, then you win a share of the prize pot. The prize pot is allocated out based on the size of your bet, i.e. someone betting £20 would get twice the return of someone betting £10.

Once our processing has been completed after the draw the winnings will be deposited into the player wallets.

Can I then have my money back?

Of course! You can withdraw your funds back to your payment provider.
